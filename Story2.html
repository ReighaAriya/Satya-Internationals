From Satya to Harvard

Muhammad Abdee Fachrezi's journey from Satya International School to becoming a Harvard University student.

Muhammad Abdee Fachrezi, a Class of 2012 alumnus of Satya International School, has achieved great success in his academic pursuits. After completing his studies at Satya, he went on to pursue his passion for learning at Harvard University. He is currently studying at Harvard, where he is excelling in his studies and making a name for himself in the academic community. Muhammad credits his success to the strong foundation he built during his time at Satya International School. He is an inspiration to many, and his story is a testament to the fact that with hard work and determination, anything is possible.


Abdee’s academic journey at Harvard University has been nothing short of remarkable. He quickly distinguished himself as a top student, earning numerous accolades for his research and academic excellence. His work in the field of quantum computing has garnered significant attention, leading to several publications in prestigious journals. In addition to his academic achievements, Abdee has been actively involved in various student organizations and initiatives. He co-founded a student-led tech innovation club, which has become a hub for aspiring technologists and innovators at Harvard. Through this club, he has mentored many students, helping them navigate their own academic and professional paths.


Abdee’s dedication to his studies and his passion for technology have also led to several internships and collaborative projects with leading tech companies. These experiences have not only enriched his knowledge but also expanded his professional network, opening doors to future opportunities. Despite his busy schedule, Abdee remains committed to giving back to his community. He frequently returns to Satya International School to speak with current students, sharing his experiences and encouraging them to pursue their dreams. His story continues to inspire many, proving that with hard work, determination, and a strong foundation, anything is possible.
